Understanding PRP & GFC

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) and Growth Factor Concentrate (GFC) are revolutionary non-surgical treatments that use a concentrated form of your own blood platelets. These platelets are rich in growth factors that, when injected into the scalp, stimulate the hair follicles, increase blood supply, and trigger natural hair growth. GFC is a more advanced, highly concentrated version of PRP that offers even more targeted results.

How the Treatment Feels

The procedure involves a standard blood draw, similar to a routine blood test. The blood is then processed in a specialized centrifuge to isolate the growth factors. The injection process is performed using very fine needles, and while you may feel a slight pinching sensation, it is generally well-tolerated. Most patients describe it as a quick and manageable procedure. There is virtually no downtime, and you can return to your daily activities immediately.

Common Questions

Hair Restoration FAQs

What is the difference between PRP and GFC?

While both use your own blood, GFC (Growth Factor Concentrate) is a purer and more advanced version. It delivers a higher concentration of active growth factors directly to the hair follicles, often yielding faster results than standard PRP.

How many sessions are needed for results?

Typically, we recommend 4-6 sessions spaced 4 weeks apart. Most patients start noticing reduced hair fall after the 2nd session and visible growth after the 4th.

Is the procedure painful?

We use ultra-fine needles and topical numbing agents to ensure the injections are comfortable. Most patients describe it as a mild tingling sensation.

Is there any downtime?

There is no downtime. You can resume your daily activities immediately. We only advise avoiding hair washing or strenuous exercise for 24 hours post-treatment.
